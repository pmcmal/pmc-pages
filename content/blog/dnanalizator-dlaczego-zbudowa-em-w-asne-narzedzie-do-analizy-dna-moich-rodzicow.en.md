---
title: DNA Analyzer: Why I Built My Own DNA Analysis Tool
date: 2026-07-22
excerpt: Instead of sending parents' DNA data to another cloud service, I wrote my own, fully local DNA analyzer — with a simple report that genuinely encourages habit changes, and a detailed one for those who want to see sources.
---

We did tests with a colleague using AncestryDNA and received raw data — a full genotype.

I thought: why not get more out of this?

Problem: ready tools are either paid or require uploading DNA data to someone's server.
These are health data of a specific person. I didn't want them to leave the disk.

I built my own tool. The assumptions were simple:
- Everything offline, locally. Zero data sending anywhere.
- Only results confirmed in scientific literature. I don't mix well-researched risky genes with trivia.
- Two reports: detailed (with gene names, sources) and simple (without jargon, encouraging changes, not just fear).

Along the way, I discovered that you can "get" to results that the test doesn't measure directly — by checking well-documented markers nearby on the genome.

The code is open. If it helps someone with their tests — that's better.
DNA and reports of course remain outside the repository.

https://github.com/pmcmal/DNAnalizator
